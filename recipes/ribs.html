Easy Fall Off the Bone Oven Baked Ribs

Description

This recipe originally uses baby back ribs but any ribs will work for the recipe. It claims to be the best ribs recipe that the author ever created! They say to, bake low and slow.

This recipe is a simple, few hour recipe. Most of it is hands off and just waiting! It's great for the busy person who only has a few minutes to cook but plenty of time to wait. Super excited for the point in time which I get the chance to try them out!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of ribs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon hot chili sauce
  • 2 tablespoons light brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and Pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 275°F
  2. Season the ribs, if the ribs have a membrane on the back, remove it.
  3. Wrap the ribs with foil by placing the ribs meatiest-side down. Cover the pan tightly with aluminum foil.
  4. Bake the ribs for 2½ hours to 3½ hours or until the meat falls off the bone.
  5. To check if they are done, pierce with a sharp knife, there should be very little resistance.
  6. Prepare the BBQ sauce by heating olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat, then, add the onions and cook them until they are soft and translucent which should take about 5 to 8 minutes.
  7. Stir in the cumin and cook for 30 more seconds. Next, add the ketchup, hot chili sauce, brown sugar, and apple cider vinegar. Stir everything to combine, season with salt, and cook for 2 minutes. Set the sauce aside while the ribs finish baking.
  8. Brush the sauce onto the ribs: Remove the ribs from the oven, discard the aluminum foil, and generously brush both sides with barbecue sauce.
  9. Broil the ribs: Move an oven rack near the top of the oven and turn your broiler to high. Broil the ribs until the BBQ sauce begins to caramelize, about 3 to 4 minutes. Keep a close eye on them during this step to ensure the sauce doesn't burn. Enjoy!
